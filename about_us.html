About Us

Our Mission

Telecom4Good seeks to develop a self-sustaining program to help NGOs reduce their direct overhead costs associated with the research, negotiation, procurement and management of their Internet services globally.

Our Passion

The true passion behind Telecom4Good is about empowering NGOs and encouraging collaboration to aggregate Internet bandwidth within areas where multiple NGOs operate. Through this group effort, Telecom4Good can negotiate significant cost savings while also improving the quality of Internet service for international NGOs.

Our Services

Telecom4Good is dedicated to help NGOs reduce or eliminate internal overhead associated with telecommunication services globally. We improve the quality and performance of member Internet services by using dedicated 1:1 contention Internet and lowering costs by aggregating the bandwidth and leveraging economies of scale through our local partners globally.

Our services include:

  • Pre-negotiated Internet service pricing with discounts of 30-55%
  • Comprehensive Internet service provider research throughout 129 countries
  • Pre-negotiated local Internet service provider contractual clauses to protect the NGO
  • Managed implementation
  • Service level agreement management
  • Managed trouble ticket escalations
  • Managed contract renewals

Participants of our Bandwidth Aggregation Program (BAP) also receive the following services:

  • Up to 80% off Cisco Meraki products to manage your LAN through the Meraki Cloud
  • FREE Cisco Refurbished Equipment Program
  • 100% Unbiased Internet service provider managed RFP program

Our Leadership  

Robert Anderson President/CEO

Robert is the president and CEO of Telecom4Good. His experience with both international professional telecommunications work and international nonprofit efforts. His background in telecommunications has given him an understanding of international business operations. His focus on improving the quality of service has also motivated him to look for ways to reduce the cost to meet the customers’ business objectives.

Robert is driven to create an environment for NGOs to leverage collaboration with other locally based NGOs in Africa. Aggregating their internet purchases will not only command higher quality internet but also greatly reduce costs. Robert has personal relationships with 300+ international NGOs with more than 2500 offices globally. In order to meet the needs of Telecom4Good’s NGO clients he has built personal relationships with more than 129 high quality Internet service providers around the world. Robert has been working with many of the largest NGOs through conferences, roundtables, webinars sponsored by InsideNGO and NetHope. This collaboration has helped further efforts to improve internet network performance and reduce overhead costs associated with research, contracting, procuring, managing and maintaining Internet services.
